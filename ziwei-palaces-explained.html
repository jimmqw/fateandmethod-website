Zi Wei Dou Shu: The 12 Palaces Explained

📅 April 16, 2026·⏱️ 10 min read

The 12 Palaces (十二宫)

In Zi Wei Dou Shu, the life chart is divided into 12 Palaces (十二宫), each representing a different aspect of life. Understanding these palaces is essential for reading your destiny.

Each palace contains stars that determine the fortune of that specific life area.

Detailed Palace Meanings

1. Life Palace
命宫 (Ming Gong)
Represents the self, physical body, personality, and overall life destiny. This is the most important palace in the chart.
2. Wealth Palace
财帛宫 (Cai Bo Gong)
Represents finances, income, wealth accumulation, and financial luck. Shows how you earn and manage money.
3. House Palace
田宅宫 (Tian Zhai Gong)
Represents property, real estate, living environment, and family assets. Also relates to stored wealth.
4. Parents Palace
父母宫 (Fu Mu Gong)
Represents parents, inheritance, education, and background. Shows relationship with parents and heritage.
5. Children Palace
子女宫 (Zi Nu Gong)
Represents children, offspring, creative expression, and legacy. Also relates to investments.
6. Health Palace
疾厄宫 (Ji E Gong)
Represents health, illness, and physical challenges. Also relates to employees and subordinates.
7. Relationship Palace
夫妻宫 (Fu Qi Gong)
Represents marriage, romantic relationships, and partnerships. Key to understanding relationship luck.
8. Siblings Palace
兄弟宫 (Xiong Di Gong)
Represents siblings, friends, and colleagues. Shows your social network and support system.
9. Fame Palace
官禄宫 (Guan Lu Gong)
Represents career, reputation, official position, and social status. Key to career analysis.
10. Fortune Palace
福德宫 (Fu De Gong)
Represents fortune, luck,享受 (enjoyment), and spiritual well-being. Shows quality of life.
11. Travel Palace
迁移宫 (Qian Yi Gong)
Represents travel, migration, external matters, and opportunities outside home country.
12. Hidden Palace
玄宫 (Xuan Gong)
Represents hidden matters, secrets, spiritual matters, and things not visible.

How to Read the Palaces

When analyzing any palace, consider:

  1. The ruling star: Which main star occupies the palace
  2. Visiting stars: Other stars that enter the palace
  3. Transformations: Whether Si Hua (四化) appears in the palace
  4. Opening and closing stars: The arms that open and close the palace

Summary

The 12 Palaces form the foundation of Zi Wei Dou Shu chart reading. Each palace provides insights into different aspects of life. By analyzing which stars occupy and affect each palace, you can understand the nuanced destiny in all areas of life.
