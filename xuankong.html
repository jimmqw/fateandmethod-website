Xuan Kong (玄空风水)
Flying Stars — The Time-Space Divination System

Where conventional feng shui reads a space, Xuan Kong reads how a space changes — which areas awaken and which decline as time flows through them.

Xuan Kong (玄空风水 — "Mysterious Space Feng Shui") represents the most sophisticated and analytically rigorous dimension of the Chinese feng shui tradition. Where most feng shui systems treat a space as fundamentally static — its orientation, its layout, its surrounding environment as fixed qualities to be assessed — Xuan Kong introduces a revolutionary concept: that the energy of any location changes over time. A room's most auspicious corner in 2024 may become its most problematic by 2032. A building's favorable entrance this decade may be unfavorable in the next. Xuan Kong maps these temporal shifts with a precision that has made it the preferred system of architects, developers, and serious feng shui practitioners throughout East Asia.

What Is Xuan Kong?

Xuan Kong is built on the principle that space and time are not separate — they are dimensions of the same phenomenon, and understanding one requires understanding the other. The system assigns a numeric value called a flying star to each of the nine palaces within a space, and these values shift on a predictable cycle governed by the Lu Pan (罗盘 — compass cycle) and the river map (河图) and Luo Shu (洛书) number sequences.

The nine palaces (九宫) correspond to the nine numbers of the Luo Shu magic square — 1 through 9 — arranged in a 3×3 grid. Each number carries an elemental nature and a set of auspicious or inauspicious qualities. The mountain dragon (山龙) flying star determines the energetic quality of the space itself; the water dragon (水龙) flying star determines the energetic quality of the space's relationship to its environment and the opportunities it draws from outside. The interaction between mountain and water stars within each palace determines whether that area of the building is currently activated, dormant, or challenged.

The Nine Palaces and Flying Stars

The nine-palace structure (九宫飞星) is the engine of Xuan Kong. Each palace contains three flying stars: the mountain star (山星), the water star (水星), and the yearly flying star (年飞星) that updates annually, along with monthly and daily stars that add further granularity for precise timing analysis.

This temporal dimension is what makes Xuan Kong uniquely predictive among feng shui systems.

Mountain Dragon and Water Dragon

The distinction between mountain dragon and water dragon is fundamental to Xuan Kong theory. The mountain dragon governs the internal energy of the space — the quality of stillness, rest, cultivation, and accumulation. The water dragon governs the external energy — the space's openness to the outside world, its capacity to draw in resources, opportunities, and connections from beyond its walls.

In practice, a good Xuan Kong reading balances both: a space with strong mountain energy but weak water energy may be stagnant (full of potential but unable to actualize it), while a space with strong water energy but weak mountain energy may be chaotic (open to many opportunities but lacking the foundation to hold onto them). The goal is balance — a space where both dragons are harmonious and mutually reinforcing.

"Conventional feng shui tells you what a space is. Xuan Kong tells you what a space will become — and when. It is the only feng shui system that treats time as a first-class variable alongside direction and location."

The 64 Houses: Configurations of Destiny

When Xuan Kong is applied to residential or commercial buildings, the resulting analysis is organized into what practitioners call the 64 House configurations (64局). Each configuration results from a specific combination of mountain star, water star, and yearly star in each of the nine palaces, and each configuration has a documented set of typical outcomes for the occupants.

These 64 configurations are not deterministic — they describe tendencies and potentials, not fixed outcomes. But they provide a remarkably detailed picture of what a particular building is likely to generate for its occupants over a given time period, and identifying which configuration a building is currently in allows the practitioner to make specific recommendations about which areas to activate, which to calm, and when to expect specific types of developments.

Choosing the Right Location

Xuan Kong is often used retrospectively to analyze an existing space, but its highest-value application is prospective — analyzing potential spaces before occupation or construction. When evaluating a location using Xuan Kong, the primary considerations are:

First, the entrance star (大门星) — the flying star occupying the palace corresponding to the building's entrance. This star has an outsized influence because the entrance is where external energy enters the space. An unfavorable entrance star can compromise an otherwise balanced configuration.

Second, the period of construction (立向) — the directional orientation of the building at the time it was built or renovated. This determines the foundational distribution of mountain and water stars across the nine palaces, which then shifts predictably over time as the cycle progresses.

Third, the current period (运) — which of the three Xuan Kong time cycles (pre-2004, 2004-2023, 2024-2043) the building is operating within. Each period shifts the entire configuration and fundamentally changes which palaces are activated and which are dormant.

Time and Space as One

The deepest principle of Xuan Kong is that time and space are not separate categories — they are two aspects of a single unified field. This is expressed in the concept of Xuan Kong itself: Xuan (玄) means mysterious, hidden, profound; Kong (空) means space, emptiness, but also time as the medium within which events occur.

In practice, this means that no direction is inherently good or bad — a southwest direction may be highly favorable in one time period and deeply problematic in another. No palace is permanently auspicious or inauspicious. The only constant is the cycle itself, and understanding the cycle is what allows the Xuan Kong practitioner to read not just where energy is, but where it is going.

Xuan Kong in Modern Real Estate

In contemporary East Asia — particularly Hong Kong, Singapore, Taiwan, and mainland China's major cities — Xuan Kong is routinely applied to real estate decisions at the highest levels. Developers analyze the Xuan Kong configuration of proposed building sites before purchase. Interior designers consult on office layouts based on which palaces correspond to which functional areas. Families moving into a new home routinely commission a Xuan Kong analysis to understand which rooms to use for which purposes and which periods will be most and least favorable for the household.

What makes this especially relevant in modern urban environments is that Xuan Kong accounts for the impact of surrounding buildings — their heights, their positions, their entrances — on the energy of a given space. In a dense city where a building's environment changes constantly as neighboring structures are built, renovated, or demolished, Xuan Kong provides a framework for understanding how these environmental changes shift the energetic profile of a space over time.

Getting Started with Xuan Kong

Begin Your Journey into Flying Star Feng Shui

  1. Understand the Luo Shu magic square — The nine-palace structure of Xuan Kong is the Luo Shu (洛书) arrangement: 4 and 9 at top, 3 and 8 at sides, 5 in the center, 2 and 7 at bottom, 1 and 6 at bottom corners. Memorize this arrangement first — everything in Xuan Kong flows from it.
  2. Learn the elemental nature of each number — 1 is water, 2 is earth (illness), 3 is wood (conflict), 4 is wood (literary), 5 is earth (neutral/transformative), 6 is metal (authority), 7 is metal (口舌是非), 8 is earth (prosperity), 9 is fire (prosperity/recognition).
  3. Study the mountain and water stars — Every palace has both a mountain star and a water star. Learn how they interact: a good mountain star with a good water star in the same palace is ideal; a good mountain star with a bad water star creates imbalance.
  4. Track the time cycle — Xuan Kong operates on 20-year sub-cycles within a 180-year grand cycle. Currently we are in the 8th period (艮宫运, 2004-2023 extended through 2043 with 9th period beginning). Know which period you are analyzing.
  5. Assess the entrance first — The entrance star (大门星) is the single most important factor in a building's Xuan Kong profile. Identify which palace your entrance falls in and which star occupies it.
  6. Apply annually and monthly — The yearly flying stars shift on li chun (立春, the beginning of spring). Monthly and daily stars add further precision for timing specific activities or events.
