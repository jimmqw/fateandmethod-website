What is Xiao Liu Ren?

Xiao Liu Ren (灏忓叚澹?, or Little Six Ren, is the fastest and most portable of all Chinese divination systems. Unlike the more complex Da Liu Ren (澶у叚澹? Great Six Ren), Xiao Liu Ren can be calculated mentally using only the current month, day, and hour —?no books, no charts, no coins. Historically favored by generals making battlefield decisions and travelers needing immediate guidance, it delivers answers in minutes. The system maps all possible situations onto six palaces (鍏), each governed by a specific star and animal, forming a remarkably consistent predictive framework.

Despite sharing "Liu Ren" in the name, Xiao Liu Ren is actually a separate, simplified system —?not a junior version of Da Liu Ren. While Da Liu Ren uses a full day-based stem-branch calculation with 12 palaces and is among the most complex Chinese divination methods, Xiao Liu Ren uses a simplified modulo-6 calculation with only 6 palaces. Think of Xiao Liu Ren as the quick reference card —?fast, directional, and surprisingly accurate.

How to Cast Xiao Liu Ren

Xiao Liu Ren casting uses the lunar calendar. The calculation can be done mentally in under 30 seconds:

  1. Note the current month (lunar month, 1–12)
  2. Note the current day (lunar day, 1–30)
  3. Note the current two-hour period (shi-chen, 1–12, starting from Zi = 23:00–1:00)
  4. Calculate: (Month + Day + Hour) ÷ 6, take the remainder (if remainder = 0, use 6)
    The remainder = Palace: 1=Shou, 2=Xue, 3=Mian, 4=Sai, 5=Saixia, 6=Yin
  5. Read the palace meaning combined with the star and animal for your answer

Quick example: Today is lunar month 3, day 15, current period is Mao (5am–7am = shi-chen 4). Calculation: (3 + 15 + 4) = 22 ÷ 6 = remainder 4 = Sai Palace (Wealth/Cheek). The answer relates to financial matters —?whether to pursue, wait, or change direction.

Xiao Liu Ren vs Da Liu Ren

Despite sharing "Liu Ren" in the name, the two systems are fundamentally different:

Da Liu Ren (澶у叚澹? uses a full day-based stem-branch calculation with 12 palaces and is among the most complex Chinese divination methods. Readings are elaborate, multi-layered, and historically used for state affairs and military strategy.

Xiao Liu Ren (灏忓叚澹? uses a simplified modulo-6 calculation with only 6 palaces. Where Da Liu Ren gives elaborate readings, Xiao Liu Ren gives quick directional answers. Think of Xiao Liu Ren as the "quick reference card" version —?not a replacement, but a complementary rapid-assessment tool.

When to Use Xiao Liu Ren

Xiao Liu Ren is best used when:

• You need a fast answer and have no tools
• You want a preliminary read before using a more complex system
• You're traveling or in an unfamiliar environment
• You need a yes/no/direction answer rather than detailed timing
• A quick gut check on a decision

Xiao Liu Ren is less suitable when:

• Precise timing questions (use Liu Yao or Xuan Kong)
• Deep personality analysis (use Zi Wei or Ba Zi)
• Complex multi-variable questions requiring nuanced analysis
• Questions where the full picture matters more than direction
