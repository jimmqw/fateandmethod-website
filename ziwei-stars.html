Zi Wei Dou Shu  /  The 14 Main Stars

The 14 Main Stars (十四主星)

Every Zi Wei Dou Shu chart is built from 14 stars. Master these, and you can read any chart.

The 14 Main Stars are the foundation of every Zi Wei Dou Shu chart. They are energetic archetypes, each carrying a distinct quality that shapes personality, behavior, and life tendencies. Understanding these 14 stars is the essential first step in learning Zi Wei Dou Shu.

1. Zi Wei (Emperor Star)

Zi Wei - The Emperor Star

Highest rank - Leadership - Authority - Ambition

Zi Wei is the most prominent star in the Zi Wei system - so important the entire system is named after it. It represents authority, leadership, ambition, and the drive to be exceptional. People with a strong Zi Wei tend to be natural leaders, often found in positions of authority or influence.

2. Tian Ji (Mechanical Star)

Tian Ji - The Mechanical Star

Intelligence - Strategy - Ideas - Planning

Tian Ji is the star of intelligence, strategy, and planning. It gives a sharp, analytical mind - someone who thinks three steps ahead and sees patterns others miss. People with a strong Tian Ji are often drawn to strategy, planning, consulting, and intellectual work.

3. Tai Yang (Sun Star)

Tai Yang - The Sun Star

Radiance - Generosity - Public Image - Visibility

Tai Yang is the star of brightness, generosity, and public standing. It makes a person generous, outward-facing, and naturally visible. People with Tai Yang prominent tend to be known, respected, and given to charity.

4. Wu Qu (Martial Wealth Star)

Wu Qu - The Martial Wealth Star

Financial acumen - Determination - Discipline - Wealth

Wu Qu is the star of financial ability, determination, and disciplined action. People with Wu Qu prominent tend to be pragmatic, action-oriented, and excellent with money management. They are builders.

5. Tian Tong (Heavenly Union Star)

Tian Tong - The Heavenly Union Star

Happiness - Ease - Diplomacy - Optimism

Tian Tong is the star of happiness, ease, and harmonious pleasure. It represents the ability to enjoy life, find contentment, and maintain a sunny disposition even in difficult circumstances.

6. Lian Zhen (Pure Star)

Lian Zhen - The Pure Star

Passion - Complexity - Contradiction - Intensity

Lian Zhen is the most paradoxical star in the system. It carries both great beauty and great danger. It represents passion, intense emotions, and complex interpersonal dynamics.

7. Tian Fu (Heavenly Wealth Star)

Tian Fu - The Heavenly Wealth Star

Prosperity - Stewardship - Calm authority - Management

Tian Fu is the natural counterpart to Zi Wei - where Zi Wei creates ambition, Tian Fu creates the ability to manage and steward what that ambition builds. It represents wealth, stability, and calm authority.

8. Tai Yin (Great Yin Star)

Tai Yin - The Great Yin Star

Intuition - Nurturing - Feminine energy - Hidden matters

Tai Yin is the star of intuition, nurturing, and the inner life. It represents the feminine principle - receptivity, sensitivity, and care. People with Tai Yin prominent tend to be deeply intuitive.

9. Tan Lang (Greedy Wolf Star)

Tan Lang - The Greedy Wolf Star

Desire - Ambition - Material drive - Social intelligence

Tan Lang is the star of desire, ambition, and material drive. It is the most socially intelligent star in the system. People with Tan Lang prominent understand exactly what others want.

10. Ju Men (Giant Gate Star)

Ju Men - The Giant Gate Star

Communication - Secrets - Investigation - Dual nature

Ju Men is the star of communication and hidden things. It represents both the ability to speak persuasively and the tendency to speak destructively. People with Ju Men prominent are often excellent speakers.

11. Tian Xiang (Prime Minister Star)

Tian Xiang - The Prime Minister Star

Diplomacy - Strategy - Support - Service

Tian Xiang is the star of diplomacy, support, and strategic service. It is the natural partner to Zi Wei - where Zi Wei is the emperor, Tian Xiang is the prime minister who executes.

12. Tian Liang (Celestial Pillar Star)

Tian Liang - The Celestial Pillar Star

Supervision - Elder support - Healing - Tradition

Tian Liang is the star of supervision, elder support, and traditional wisdom. It represents the elder who guides, the mentor who protects, and the system that preserves order.

13. Qi Sha (Seven Killings Star)

Qi Sha - The Seven Killings Star

Destruction - Courage - Military - Sudden change

Qi Sha is the star of destruction, courage, and radical transformation. It represents the capacity to destroy and rebuild. People with Qi Sha prominent tend to be decisive and courageous.

14. Po Jun (Destroyer Star)

Po Jun - The Destroyer Star

Destruction - Innovation - Revolution - Persistence

Po Jun is the final and most extreme of the 14 stars - the destroyer, the revolutionary, the force that razes the old to make way for the new. It represents absolute destruction in service of transformation.

"The 14 stars are not predictions. They are tendencies. Every person has all 14 stars in their chart - the question is which ones are prominent, which are weak, and which interact with each other."
