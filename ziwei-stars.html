Zi Wei Dou Shu  /  The 14 Main Stars

The 14 Main Stars (十四主星)

Every Zi Wei Dou Shu chart is built from 14 stars. Master these, and you can read any chart.

The 14 Main Stars (十四主星) are the foundation of every Zi Wei Dou Shu chart. They are not planets in the astronomical sense — they are energetic archetypes, each carrying a distinct quality that shapes personality, behavior, and life tendencies. Understanding these 14 stars is the essential first step in learning Zi Wei Dou Shu. You can think of them as the vocabulary: without mastering this vocabulary, the grammar of chart reading is inaccessible.

1. 紫微 — The Emperor Star (Zǐ Wēi)

紫微星 — The Emperor Star

Highest rank · Leadership · Authority · Ambition

The Emperor Star is the most prominent star in the Zi Wei system — so important the entire system is named after it. It represents authority, leadership, ambition, and the drive to be exceptional. People with a strong 紫微 tend to be natural leaders, often found in positions of authority or influence. But 紫微 alone is not enough: without supporting stars, it can create arrogance or loneliness. The ideal configuration: 紫微 paired with 天府 (the wealth star) or 天相 (the prime minister star), creating a balanced expression of power and diplomacy.

2. 天机 — The Mechanical Star (Tiān Jī)

天机星 — The Mechanical Star

Intelligence · Strategy · Ideas · Planning

天机 is the star of intelligence, strategy, and planning. It gives a sharp, analytical mind — someone who thinks three steps ahead and sees patterns others miss. People with a strong 天机 are often drawn to strategy, planning, consulting, and intellectual work. It is a "scholar's star" — it favors the life of the mind over physical labor. The caution: 天机 people can overthink, get lost in analysis paralysis, or become emotionally detached in favor of pure logic.

3. 太阳 — The Sun Star (Tài Yáng)

太阳星 — The Sun Star

Radiance · Generosity · Public Image · Visibility

太阳 is the star of brightness, generosity, and public standing. It makes a person generous, outward-facing, and naturally visible — the kind of person others look to in a crowd. 太阳 in the right palace creates someone who is known, respected, and given to charity and public service. The challenge: 太阳 can create a performative quality, where actions are motivated by external validation rather than internal conviction. In the wrong configuration, it can lead to ego inflation or generosity that depletes oneself.

4. 武曲 — The Martial Wealth Star (Wǔ Qū)

武曲星 — The Martial Wealth Star

Financial acumen · Determination · Discipline · Wealth

武曲 is the star of financial ability, determination, and disciplined action. Where 天机 thinks and 太阳 shines, 武曲 does. People with 武曲 prominent in their chart tend to be pragmatic, action-oriented, and excellent with money management. They are not dreamers — they are builders. 武曲 is particularly strong in the 财帛宫 (Wealth Palace) and 田宅宫 (Property Palace), where it indicates accumulation of material assets. The caution: 武曲 alone can create rigidity or an overly materialistic orientation.

5. 天同 — The Heavenly Union Star (Tiān Tóng)

天同星 — The Heavenly Union Star

Happiness · Ease · Diplomacy · Optimism

天同 is the star of happiness, ease, and harmonious pleasure. It represents the ability to enjoy life, find contentment, and maintain a sunny disposition even in difficult circumstances. People with 天同 prominent tend to be diplomatic, easy to be around, and naturally optimistic. It is a nurturing, childlike quality — not naive, but genuinely capable of seeing the positive. The caution: 天同 can indicate resistance to hard work, or a tendency to seek comfort over growth.

6. 廉贞 — The Pure Chastity Star (Lián Zhēn)

廉贞星 — The Pure Chastity Star

Passion · Complexity · Contradiction · Intensity

廉贞 is the most paradoxical star in the system — it carries both great beauty and great danger. It represents passion, intense emotions, and complex interpersonal dynamics. 廉贞 is associated with romantic love, emotional depth, and the capacity for both profound devotion and destructive obsession. In the right configuration, it creates someone deeply feeling, artistically gifted, and capable of great love. In the wrong configuration, it can create emotional manipulation, jealousy, or self-destructive patterns.

7. 天府 — The Heavenly Wealth Star (Tiān Fǔ)

天府星 — The Heavenly Wealth Star

Prosperity · Stewardship · Calm authority · Management

天府 is the natural counterpart to 紫微 — where 紫微 creates ambition, 天府 creates the ability to manage and steward what that ambition builds. It represents wealth, stability, and calm authority. People with 天府 prominent are often administrators, managers, or stewards — people who preserve and grow rather than create and destroy. 天府 is considered one of the most favorable stars in the system: it brings stability, protection, and the ability to weather difficult periods.

8. 太阴 — The Great Yin Star (Tài Yīn)

太阴星 — The Great Yin Star

Intuition · Nurturing · Feminine energy · Hidden matters

太阴 is the star of intuition, nurturing, and the inner life. It represents the feminine principle — not gender-specific, but the quality of receptivity, sensitivity, and care. People with 太阴 prominent tend to be deeply intuitive, emotionally intelligent, and drawn to nurturing roles. 太阴 is also associated with hidden matters — it governs secrets, private affairs, and the parts of life that happen behind closed doors. In women's charts, it often emphasizes beauty and artistic sensitivity.

9. 贪狼 — The Greedy Wolf Star (Tān Láng)

贪狼星 — The Greedy Wolf Star

Desire · Ambition · Material drive · Social intelligence

贪狼 is the star of desire, ambition, and material drive. It is the most socially intelligent star in the system — people with 贪狼 prominent understand exactly what others want and how to give it to them. This makes 贪狼 excellent for business, sales, diplomacy, and entertainment. But 贪狼 also represents excess: the drive to consume, acquire, and possess that can become destructive. In the best configuration, it creates a person of extraordinary social charm and the ambition to achieve great things legitimately.

The Transformation of 贪狼

The quality of 贪狼 shifts dramatically based on its Four Transformations (四化):

· 贪狼化禄 — Desire meets abundance. Social charm converts to financial success.

· 贪狼化权 — Desire becomes control. Ambition sharpens into the ability to manage complex situations and people.

· 贪狼化忌 — Desire becomes obsession. The dark side emerges: possessiveness, jealousy, addiction, corruption.

10. 巨门 — The Giant Gate Star (Jù Mén)

巨门星 — The Giant Gate Star

Communication · Secrets · Investigation · Dual nature

巨门 is the star of the mouth, communication, and hidden things. It represents both the ability to speak persuasively and the tendency to speak destructively. People with 巨门 prominent are often excellent speakers, writers, or investigators — they have access to information others do not, and the ability to communicate it compellingly. But 巨门 also governs secrets, lies, and the hidden dimensions of situations. Its dual nature means it can create either profound wisdom or deep deception, depending on the rest of the chart.

11. 天相 — The Prime Minister Star (Tiān Xiàng)

天相星 — The Prime Minister Star

Diplomacy · Strategy · Support · Service

天相 is the star of diplomacy, support, and strategic service. It is the natural partner to 紫微 — where 紫微 is the emperor, 天相 is the prime minister who executes and manages. People with 天相 prominent are often in supporting roles that carry real power: advisors, executives, managers who serve as the essential bridge between leadership and execution. 天相 is associated with justice, fairness, and the ability to mediate disputes. It is considered highly favorable when it appears alongside the major stars.

12. 天梁 — The Celestial Pillar Star (Tiān Liáng)

天梁星 — The Celestial Pillar Star

Supervision · Elder support · Healing · Tradition

天梁 is the star of supervision, elder support, and traditional wisdom. It represents the elder who guides, the mentor who protects, and the system that preserves order. People with 天梁 prominent tend to be drawn to roles of guardianship, supervision, or care for the elderly. 天梁 is also associated with healing — particularly in the medical traditions, as it represents the body's self-repair mechanisms. In terms of longevity and elderliness, 天梁 is considered one of the strongest positive indicators in the system.

13. 七杀 — The Seven Killings Star (Qī Shā)

七杀星 — The Seven Killings Star

Destruction · Courage · Military · Sudden change

七杀 is the star of destruction, courage, and radical transformation. It represents the capacity to destroy and rebuild — whether literally (military careers, emergency medicine) or psychologically (breaking down old patterns to make space for new ones). People with 七杀 prominent tend to be decisive, courageous, and capable of taking massive action when necessary. But 七杀 is unpredictable: it can bring sudden success or sudden disaster, depending on the configuration. It is not a star for the cautious.

14. 破军 — The Destroyer Star (Pò Jūn)

破军星 — The Destroyer Star

Destruction · Innovation · Revolution · Persistence

破军 is the final and most extreme of the 14 stars — the destroyer, the revolutionary, the force that razes the old to make way for the new. It represents absolute destruction in service of absolute transformation. People with 破军 prominent tend to live lives of extraordinary extremes: careers that burn brightly then collapse, relationships that consume everything, journeys that take them to the edge of experience. In the right configuration, 破军 creates pioneers — people who go where no one has gone before and have the will to survive what they find there.

"The 14 stars are not predictions. They are tendencies. Every person has all 14 stars in their chart — the question is which ones are prominent, which are weak, and which interact with each other in ways that modify their expression. That interaction is where the real art of Zi Wei reading begins."
