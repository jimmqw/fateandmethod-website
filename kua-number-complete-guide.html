How to Calculate and Use Your Kua Number

A complete guide to finding your personal Feng Shui directions — where to sleep, work, and face for better energy, relationships, and success.

Home / Feng Shui / Kua Number Guide
Of all the tools in the Feng Shui practitioner's kit, the Kua number is perhaps the most personal — and the most immediately useful. Unlike flying star charts or annual afflictions that change with time, your Kua number is fixed for life. It tells you exactly which compass directions support your well-being and which ones work against it, so you can align your bed, desk, and front door with the energies that nourish you.

What Is a Kua Number?

The Kua number (also spelled Gua or Kwa) comes from the Eight Mansions school of Feng Shui, one of the oldest and most practical branches of the tradition. It is derived from your year of birth and gender, and it assigns you to either the East Group or the West Group — a classification that determines which of the eight cardinal and intercardinal directions are your allies and which are your antagonists.

The system is built on the Ba Gua (Eight Trigrams) of the I Ching. Each Kua number corresponds to one of the eight trigrams, and each trigram has a natural affinity with four directions and a natural aversion to the other four. When you know your Kua number, you know which directions to face when you sleep, work, study, and enter your home.

Why Your Kua Number Matters

The premise is simple but profound: certain directions harmonize with your personal energy field, bringing vitality, clarity, and good fortune. Other directions create friction — draining your energy, clouding your judgment, or inviting arguments and obstacles. By aligning yourself with your auspicious directions, you work with your environment rather than against it.

Step 1: Calculate Your Kua Number

The calculation uses your lunar birth year and gender. For those born before the Chinese New Year (late January to mid-February), use the previous solar year. Here is the formula for those born in 2000 or later:

For Males (Born 2000+)

(9 − Sum of Last Two Digits of Birth Year) mod 9

If the sum is a single-digit number, use it directly. If it is two digits, add those digits together first.

If the result is 0, your Kua number is 9. If the result is 5, males use 2.

Example: Male born 2004 → 0+4=4 → 9−4=5 → 5 becomes 2

Kua number: 2 (West Group)

For Females (Born 2000+)

(Sum of Last Two Digits of Birth Year + 6) mod 9

If the sum is a single-digit number, use it directly. If it is two digits, add those digits together first.

If the result is 0, your Kua number is 9. If the result is 5, females use 8.

Example: Female born 2001 → 0+1=1 → 1+6=7 → Kua 7

Kua number: 7 (West Group)

For Those Born Before 2000

Male: (10 − Sum of Last Two Digits) mod 9  |  Female: (Sum + 5) mod 9

The same rules apply: if the result is 0, use 9; if 5, males use 2, females use 8.

Example: Male born 1987 → 8+7=15 → 1+5=6 → 10−6=4 → Kua 4

Kua number: 4 (East Group)

Step 2: Are You East or West Group?

All Kua numbers fall into one of two groups. Your group determines which set of four directions are auspicious for you:

Notice that Kua number 5 does not exist in practice — it always maps to 2 (males) or 8 (females). This is because 5 represents the center in the Luo Shu magic square, and there is no trigram associated with the center.

Step 3: Your Four Auspicious Directions

Each of your four auspicious directions carries a specific type of energy. They are ranked from most to least powerful:

1. Sheng Chi (Success & Prosperity)

Your best direction. Face this direction when working at your desk, negotiating important deals, or making key decisions. This direction attracts vitality, respect, and financial success. Ideally, your front door should face this direction, and your desk should be oriented toward it.

2. Tian Yi (Health & Vitality)

Your healing direction. Face this direction when eating meals, recovering from illness, or when you simply need to recharge. Sleep with the crown of your head pointing toward Tian Yi for restorative rest and long-term health.

3. Nien Yen (Relationships & Harmony)

Your relationship direction. Face this direction when having important conversations with your partner, family members, or close friends. This direction fosters harmony, understanding, and the deepening of personal bonds. It is also associated with longevity in marriage and family.

4. Fu Wei (Stability & Peace)

Your calming direction. This is the gentlest of the four auspicious directions. Face it when meditating, studying, or doing creative work that requires patience and focus. It promotes mental clarity and emotional steadiness rather than dramatic gains.

Step 4: Your Four Inauspicious Directions

These are the directions that create resistance in your life. Avoid facing them for extended periods — especially while sleeping or working:

1. Chueh Ming (Total Loss) — Most Dangerous

The worst direction for your Kua number. Associated with catastrophic loss, severe illness, and complete misfortune. Never sleep with your head pointing toward Chueh Ming, and avoid having your main door face this direction at all costs.

2. Wu Kwei (Five Ghosts)

Brings gossip, betrayal, fire-related accidents, and backstabbing. Associated with interpersonal turmoil — colleagues turning against you, friends spreading rumors, partners becoming unreliable. Avoid facing this direction during meetings or negotiations.

3. Lui Shar (Six Killings)

Linked to legal problems, lawsuits, chronic illness, and stagnation. If your bed faces Lui Shar, you may experience persistent health issues that resist treatment. This direction also correlates with professional dead-ends and bureaucratic obstacles.

4. Ho Hai (Misfortune)

The mildest of the inauspicious directions, but still worth avoiding. Associated with minor accidents, misunderstandings, financial leakage, and general bad luck. You won't be ruined by Ho Hai, but you'll accumulate small frustrations that drain your patience over time.

How to Apply Your Kua Number in Daily Life

Sleeping Direction

This is the single most important application of your Kua number. You spend roughly one-third of your life asleep, and the orientation of your body during those hours has a cumulative effect on your energy. The crown of your head should point toward either your Tian Yi (health) or Sheng Chi (success) direction. If neither is practical due to room layout, Nien Yen (relationships) is an acceptable alternative.

Work Desk Orientation

When seated at your desk, you should face your Sheng Chi direction — meaning your eyes look toward it. This is more important than the direction your desk is placed in the room. If you cannot face Sheng Chi, face Tian Yi or Nien Yen. Never work while facing Chueh Ming or Wu Kwei.

Front Door Direction

In Eight Mansions Feng Shui, the direction your front door faces is critical because this is where energy enters your home. If your front door faces one of your auspicious directions — especially Sheng Chi — the entire household benefits. If it faces an inauspicious direction, Feng Shui remedies (such as a Bagua mirror above the door or strategic use of the five elements) can mitigate the effect.

Eating and Socializing

When dining with family or hosting guests, face your Nien Yen direction to strengthen bonds. When eating alone, Tian Yi supports digestive health and nutrient absorption.

A Crucial Distinction

The Kua number system is about personal directions — the orientation of your body relative to the compass. Do not confuse this with the house Kua, which is determined by the sitting and facing directions of the building itself. A house that faces your inauspicious direction may still be a good home if your bed and desk are correctly oriented. The personal always takes priority over the architectural.

A Kua 9 person (Fire) needs good lighting and warmth, and should avoid excessive water elements that dampen their innate fire energy.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Using the wrong year. If you were born in January or early February, check the Chinese New Year date for your birth year. The lunar new year does not align with January 1. A person born January 15, 1990, may need to use 1989 for the calculation if the lunar new year had not yet occurred.

Confusing facing direction with sleeping direction. When you sleep, what matters is where the crown of your head points, not where your face looks. If your head points north, you are sleeping in a north direction regardless of whether your face looks east or west.

Over-relying on one direction. The four auspicious directions serve different purposes. Sleeping in Sheng Chi is excellent for ambition, but it can be overstimulating. Tian Yi is gentler for rest. Use them according to the activity, not as a one-size-fits-all rule.

Ignoring the physical environment. Your Kua number is one tool among many. If your Sheng Chi direction faces a brick wall or a toilet, the direction's energy is compromised by the physical reality. Prioritize fresh air, natural light, and uncluttered space first — then fine-tune with Kua directions.

"The Kua number is not fate. It is a compass — not the destination. It shows you the favorable winds, but you must still raise the sails."
