The 12 Palaces (鍗佷簩瀹?

Every life domain mapped: from personality to wealth to relationships, each palace tells a specific story within your Zi Wei chart.

In Zi Wei Dou Shu, the chart is divided into twelve sections called palaces (瀹?. Each palace governs a specific life domain - personality, relationships, career, wealth, health, travel, subordinates, official career, property, happiness, and parents. The placement of stars within each palace, and the relationships between palaces, are what create the chart's richness. Understanding the twelve palaces is the essential framework for reading any Zi Wei Dou Shu chart.

1. Ming Gong (Life Palace)

Life Palace (Ming Gong)

The most important palace - your core identity

The Life Palace is the most important palace in the entire chart. It represents your core personality, your essential nature, and the fundamental tendencies that define you. The stars placed in your Life Palace determine your basic character: your strengths, your weaknesses, your innate capabilities, and your primary psychological patterns.

2. Cai Bo Gong (Wealth Palace)

Wealth Palace (Cai Bo Gong)

Money, financial habits, and income sources

The Wealth Palace describes your relationship with money: how you earn it, how you spend it, and how you accumulate or lose it. A well-starred Wealth Palace suggests strong financial acumen. This palace also governs your attitude toward wealth.

3. Tian Zhai Gong (Property Palace)

Property Palace (Tian Zhai Gong)

Property, family inheritance, and domestic environment

The Property Palace governs real estate, inherited assets, and your domestic environment. It describes your relationship with property ownership, your family home, and the inheritance you receive or leave behind.

4. Fu Mu Gong (Parents Palace)

Parents Palace (Fu Mu Gong)

Parents, bosses, and authority figures

Beyond parents, this palace governs your relationship with authority figures, bosses, mentors, and the people who have power over your circumstances. It describes how authority affects your life.

5. Zi Nu Gong (Children Palace)

Children Palace (Zi Nu Gong)

Children, creativity, and legacies

Beyond children, this palace governs creativity, artistic expression, and the legacy you leave behind. The stars here describe your relationship with children, your creative drives, and your capacity to mentor the next generation.

6. Ji Wu Gong (Health Palace)

Health Palace (Ji Wu Gong)

Health, illness tendencies, and physical body

The Health Palace maps your body's vulnerabilities and tendencies. While it is not a substitute for medical diagnosis, it does indicate which organ systems are more prone to imbalance in your particular energetic makeup.

7. Qi Fu Gong (Marriage Palace)

Marriage Palace (Qi Fu Gong)

Marriage, romantic partnerships, and intimate relationships

The Marriage Palace is one of the most studied palaces in Zi Wei Dou Shu. It describes your approach to marriage and romantic relationships - not just the type of partner you attract, but your own capacity for intimacy.

8. Xiong Di Gong (Siblings Palace)

Siblings Palace (Xiong Di Gong)

Siblings, close friends, and financial partners

Despite its name, the Siblings Palace governs more than just siblings - it also indicates your relationship with close friends, colleagues who feel like siblings, and financial partners.

9. Guan Lu Gong (Career Palace)

Career Palace (Guan Lu Gong)

Career, ambition, and official standing

The Career Palace governs your professional life: your career direction, your ambitions, and your official standing. The stars here indicate which professional environments suit you.

10. Fu Bao Gong (Fortune Palace)

Fortune Palace (Fu Bao Gong)

Happiness, longevity, and spiritual fortune

The Fortune Palace is associated with overall life fortune, happiness, and longevity. It is one of the most favorable palaces to have well-starred, as it indicates a life where joy and good fortune are natural companions.

11. Qian Yi Gong (Travel Palace)

Travel Palace (Qian Yi Gong)

Travel, external circumstances, and public image

The Travel Palace governs how you are perceived outside your home environment - by strangers, in foreign places, and in the wider world.

12. Xuan Gong (Hidden Palace)

Hidden Palace (Xuan Gong)

Hidden matters, spiritual concerns, and secrets

The Hidden Palace represents hidden matters, spiritual concerns, and secrets. It also governs your inner psychological life and things you keep private from others.

The Palace Sequence and Life Cycle

In Zi Wei Dou Shu, the twelve palaces are read in a specific temporal sequence: the Life Palace governs early life, the Siblings and Marriage Palaces govern young adulthood, the Wealth and Career Palaces govern middle life, and the Property and Fortune Palaces govern later life.

"The palaces are not separate worlds - they are interconnected. A challenge in your Career Palace often creates opportunities in your Wealth Palace. A difficulty in your Marriage Palace can deepen the wisdom in your Fortune Palace."
