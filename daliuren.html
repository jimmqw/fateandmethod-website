China's Oldest Imperial Divination System

In the hierarchy of Chinese metaphysics, three systems stand above all others —?so profound, so complex, and so historically significant that they are known simply as The Three Greats (涓夊紡). Da Liu Ren, Xuan Kong, and Tai Yi. Of these three, Da Liu Ren holds the position of honor as the oldest, a system so ancient that some scholars believe it predates the Zhou Dynasty itself. For more than two thousand years, it was the exclusive preserve of imperial decision-makers —?generals, ministers, emperors —?consulted only when the fate of nations hung in the balance.

What Is Da Liu Ren?

Da Liu Ren is a day-based divination system that maps the energetic interactions between the ten Heavenly Stems (澶╁共) and twelve Earthly Branches (鍦版敮) to generate a richly detailed picture of any situation. Unlike systems that use birth data (like Ba Zi) or require ritual implements (like Liu Yao coin divination), Da Liu Ren begins from the day of the reading itself —?the day is the container of the energy, and everything in the reading flows from understanding what kind of day it is.

The system generates a structure called the Pan (鐩? —?a chart or board —?that organizes the reading into layers. The outermost layer represents heaven, the middle layer represents earth, and the inner layer represents human affairs. Within and across these layers, specific "palaces" (瀹? are activated, each governed by specific deities, each carrying specific meanings related to the question at hand.

"Where other systems read the stars of birth or the coins of the moment, Da Liu Ren reads the day itself —?the very fabric of time in which your question exists. It is as if the universe set the stage before you arrived, and Da Liu Ren reads the set design."

The level of detail in a Da Liu Ren chart is extraordinary. Each reading contains information about the twelve Earthly Branches, the ten Heavenly Stems, specific cosmic officers (绁? assigned to each position, and their complex relational dynamics. A skilled Da Liu Ren reading can identify not just the likely outcome of a situation, but the specific people involved, the timing of developments, the hidden factors at play, and the most advantageous course of action —?all rendered in a language of cosmological precision.

Getting Started with Da Liu Ren

Begin your journey into this ancient imperial system with these foundational steps:

  • Identify the day stem and branch —?Da Liu Ren begins with the day of the reading. Look up the Heavenly Stem and Earthly Branch for today's date. This is your energetic anchor —?the container within which your question exists.
  • Learn the twelve Earthly Branches —?The twelve branches (Zi, Chou, Yin, Mao, Chen, Si, Wu, Wei, Shen, You, Xu, Hai) each carry specific meanings and associations. Memorize their elemental correspondences and directional associations first.
  • Understand the ten Heavenly Stems —?The ten stems (Jia, Yi, Bing, Ding, Wu, Ji, Geng, Xin, Ren, Gui) interact with the branches to generate the Pan structure. Focus on their elemental nature and relational dynamics.
  • Study the three layers of the Pan —?Heaven, Earth, and Human layers each organize the twelve branches differently. Understanding how a branch changes its expression across layers is central to Da Liu Ren interpretation.
  • Learn the twelve palace meanings —?Each position in the Pan carries a specific life domain: career, wealth, health, relationships. Know which palace governs which domain before attempting interpretation.
  • Keep systematic records —?Record every reading with the full Pan structure, your question, the answer, and the follow-up. Da Liu Ren rewards patience and long-term pattern recognition.
