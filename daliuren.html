Da Liu Ren 大六壬

The Great Six Ren — China's Oldest Imperial Divination System

大六壬

In the hierarchy of Chinese metaphysics, three systems stand above all others — so profound, so complex, and so historically significant that they are known simply as The Three Greats (三式). Da Liu Ren, Xuan Kong, and Tai Yi. Of these three, Da Liu Ren holds the position of honor as the oldest, a system so ancient that some scholars believe it predates the Zhou Dynasty itself. For more than two thousand years, it was the exclusive preserve of imperial decision-makers — generals, ministers, emperors — consulted only when the fate of nations hung in the balance.

The name itself rewards examination. Da (大) means "great." Liu (六) means "six." Ren (壬) is the ninth of the ten Heavenly Stems in the Chinese cosmological system — a concept so fundamental to Chinese thought that it is nearly untranslatable. Ren corresponds to the element Water in its most yang form, associated with the northern direction, the winter season, the color black, and the quality of cunning intelligence. To call a system "The Great Nine Stem" is to announce, from the very first word, that one is working with forces of the deepest cosmological significance.

What Is Da Liu Ren?

Da Liu Ren is a day-based divination system that maps the energetic interactions between the ten Heavenly Stems (天干) and twelve Earthly Branches (地支) to generate a richly detailed picture of any situation. Unlike systems that use birth data (like Ba Zi) or require ritual implements (like Liu Yao coin divination), Da Liu Ren begins from the day of the reading itself — the day is the container of the energy, and everything in the reading flows from understanding what kind of day it is.

The system generates a structure called the Pan (盘) — a chart or board — that organizes the reading into layers. The outermost layer represents heaven, the middle layer represents earth, and the inner layer represents human affairs. Within and across these layers, specific "palaces" (宫) are activated, each governed by specific deities, each carrying specific meanings related to the question at hand.

"Where other systems read the stars of birth or the coins of the moment, Da Liu Ren reads the day itself — the very fabric of time in which your question exists. It is as if the universe set the stage before you arrived, and Da Liu Ren reads the set design."

The level of detail in a Da Liu Ren chart is extraordinary. Each reading contains information about the twelve Earthly Branches, the ten Heavenly Stems, specific cosmic officers (神) assigned to each position, and their complex relational dynamics. A skilled Da Liu Ren reading can identify not just the likely outcome of a situation, but the specific people involved, the timing of developments, the hidden factors at play, and the most advantageous course of action — all rendered in a language of cosmological precision.
