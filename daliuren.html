The Three Greats  /  Da Liu Ren

Da Liu Ren (大六壬)
China's Oldest Imperial Divination System

The Great Six Ren — for more than two thousand years, consulted only when the fate of nations hung in the balance.

In the hierarchy of Chinese metaphysics, three systems stand above all others — so profound, so complex, and so historically significant that they are known simply as The Three Greats (三式). Da Liu Ren, Xuan Kong, and Tai Yi. Of these three, Da Liu Ren holds the position of honor as the oldest, a system so ancient that some scholars believe it predates the Zhou Dynasty itself. For more than two thousand years, it was the exclusive preserve of imperial decision-makers — generals, ministers, emperors — consulted only when the fate of nations hung in the balance.

What Is Da Liu Ren?

Da Liu Ren (大六壬, "Great Six Ren") is a day-based divination system that maps the energetic interactions between the ten Heavenly Stems (十天干) and the twelve Earthly Branches (十二地支) to generate a richly detailed picture of any situation. Unlike systems that use birth data (like Ba Zi) or require ritual implements (like Liu Yao coin divination), Da Liu Ren begins from the day of the reading itself — the day is the container of the energy, and everything in the reading flows from understanding what kind of day it is.

The system generates a structure called the Pan (盘 — a chart or board) that organizes the reading into layers. The outermost layer represents heaven, the middle layer represents earth, and the inner layer represents human affairs. Within and across these layers, specific palaces are activated, each governed by specific deities, each carrying specific meanings related to the question at hand.

"Where other systems read the stars of birth or the coins of the moment, Da Liu Ren reads the day itself — the very fabric of time in which your question exists. It is as if the universe set the stage before you arrived, and Da Liu Ren reads the set design."

The level of detail in a Da Liu Ren chart is extraordinary. Each reading contains information about the twelve Earthly Branches, the ten Heavenly Stems, specific cosmic officers assigned to each position, and their complex relational dynamics. A skilled Da Liu Ren reading can identify not just the likely outcome of a situation, but the specific people involved, the timing of developments, the hidden factors at play, and the most advantageous course of action — all rendered in a language of cosmological precision.

The Pan: Three Layers of Reality

The foundation of every Da Liu Ren reading is the Pan — a three-dimensional conceptual map of reality organized into Heaven, Earth, and Human layers. Each layer reveals a different dimension of the situation:

The Heaven layer reflects the broader cosmic currents and general trends — what is moving in the environment beyond individual control. The Earth layer shows the geographical and circumstantial factors at play — the terrain, as it were, within which events unfold. The Human layer reveals the personal dynamics, motivations, and actions of the people involved.

What makes Da Liu Ren remarkably precise is its use of twelve earthly branch positions as the structural skeleton of the chart. Each branch carries its own elemental nature, directional association, and symbolic meaning, and these shift and interact differently across each of the three layers — creating an extraordinarily nuanced picture of any situation.

Da Liu Ren Within The Three Greats

The Three Greats (三式) represent the pinnacle of Chinese metaphysical systems. Together they form a complete triad: Da Liu Ren addresses tian shi (heavenly timing and destiny), Xuan Kong addresses kong jian (spatial and environmental timing), and Tai Yi addresses ren shi (human affairs and relationships).

Of the three, Da Liu Ren is considered the most suitable for questions about personal destiny, relationship dynamics, and situations where the human element is paramount. It excels when the question centers on understanding the true intentions of others, predicting how a situation will unfold, or identifying hidden factors that are influencing a matter.

Getting Started with Da Liu Ren

Begin Your Journey Into This Ancient Imperial System

  1. Identify the day stem and branch — Da Liu Ren begins with the day of the reading. Look up the Heavenly Stem and Earthly Branch for today's date. This is your energetic anchor — the container within which your question exists.
  2. Learn the twelve Earthly Branches — The twelve branches (Zi, Chou, Yin, Mao, Chen, Si, Wu, Wei, Shen, You, Xu, Hai) each carry specific meanings and associations. Memorize their elemental correspondences and directional associations first.
  3. Understand the ten Heavenly Stems — The ten stems (Jia, Yi, Bing, Ding, Wu, Ji, Geng, Xin, Ren, Gui) interact with the branches to generate the Pan structure. Focus on their elemental nature and relational dynamics — particularly the Six Relations (生克冲合害).
  4. Study the three layers of the Pan — Heaven, Earth, and Human layers each organize the twelve branches differently. Understanding how a branch changes its expression across layers is central to Da Liu Ren interpretation.
  5. Learn the twelve palace meanings — Each position in the Pan carries a specific life domain: career, wealth, health, relationships. Know which palace governs which domain before attempting interpretation.
  6. Keep systematic records — Record every reading with the full Pan structure, your question, the answer, and the follow-up. Da Liu Ren rewards patience and long-term pattern recognition above all else.

The Six Relations: How Energy Interacts

The dynamic between stems and branches in Da Liu Ren is governed by the Six Relations (六亲生克冲合害) — six types of energetic interaction that describe how different elements relate to one another. These range from harmonious support (生, Sheng — promotion) and constructive control (克, Ke — restriction) to more subtle tensions like approaching harm (害, Hai — concealed obstruction).

Mastering these six relations is essential to reading the chart accurately. They determine whether a palace is strengthened or weakened by its neighbors, whether the officers within it are in harmony or conflict, and ultimately whether the indicated outcome is favorable or adverse.

Who Was Da Liu Ren For?

Historically, Da Liu Ren was the exclusive tool of imperial decision-makers. Generals planning campaigns would consult it to determine optimal timing and strategy. Ministers would use it to assess the intentions of rival factions. Emperors would turn to it when the stability of the realm itself was at stake.

This is not a system for casual curiosity. Its traditional applications centered on matters of genuine consequence — questions where the stakes were high, the variables numerous, and the need for precision acute. This heritage gives Da Liu Ren its distinctive character: a system built for depth, not breadth; for understanding the particular dynamics of a specific situation, not general life guidance.

Today, Da Liu Ren remains one of the most sophisticated divination systems in existence. Its combination of day-stem mapping, three-layer analysis, and multi-officer chart structure offers a level of nuance that few systems can match — making it ideal for anyone seeking genuine insight into complex situations where superficial answers will not suffice.
