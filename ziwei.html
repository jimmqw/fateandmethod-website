Zi Wei Dou Shu (紫微斗数)

China's most sophisticated astrological system. 12 palaces, 14 main stars, and 100+ auxiliary stars create a complete map of your destiny.

紫微斗数

Zi Wei Dou Shu — "Purple Forbidden Star Number System" — is one of the three great Chinese destiny systems (三式), alongside Da Liu Ren and Tai Yi. Where most divination systems answer a specific question, Zi Wei maps the entirety of a life: personality structure, relationship patterns, career aptitude, wealth cycles, health tendencies, and the timing of major life transitions. Its twelve palaces allow it to analyze not just what will happen, but how you will experience it — the specific texture and relationship context that other systems cannot reach.

The 12 Palaces

命宫 (Life), 兄弟, 夫妻, 子女, 财帛, 疾厄, 迁移, 奴仆, 官禄, 田宅, 福德, 父母 — each palace maps a distinct life domain with its own star occupants and energetic quality.

The 14 Main Stars

紫微, 天机, 太阳, 武曲, 天同, 廉贞, 天府, 太阴, 贪狼, 巨门, 天相, 天梁, 七杀, 破军 — each an archetypal personality force that shapes how its palace expresses itself.

Four Transformations

禄 (Prosperity), 权 (Power), 科 (Noble), 忌 (Obstacle) — four modes of energetic activation that mark where energy is intensified, stabilized, or challenged in your chart.

Why Birth Time Matters

Zi Wei is uniquely sensitive to the birth hour. Different hours place different stars in different palaces — making the exact time more critical here than in almost any other Chinese system.

Zi Wei vs. Ba Zi — What's the Difference?

Ba Zi tells you what your tendencies are and when your favorable and unfavorable periods arrive. It maps the road.

Zi Wei maps the person driving — their personality structure, the specific quality of their relationships, the precise way they interact with the world. It goes deeper into relational and psychological detail.

The two systems complement each other: Ba Zi for timing, Zi Wei for depth. Explore Ba Zi or read the full comparison in our introduction.
