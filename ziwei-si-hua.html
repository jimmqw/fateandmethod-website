Zi Wei Dou Shu: The Four Transformations

📅 April 16, 2026·⏱️ 8 min read

What Are the Four Transformations (Si Hua)?

The Four Transformations, known as Si Hua (四化), are the core concept in Zi Wei Dou Shu astrology. They include Lu (化禄 - Prosperity), Quan (化权 - Power), Ke (化科 - Noble), and Ji (化忌 - Obstacle).

These transformations originate from the interaction of Tian Gan (Heavenly Stems) and Di Zhi (Earthly Branches), determining the fortune changes in each palace of the life chart.

1. Lu (化禄) - Prosperity

Nature: Prosperity, wealth, income, material enjoyment

  • Represents salary, investments, business income
  • Represents material comfort and lifestyle
  • Represents romantic relationships and social luck
  • Represents financial opportunities

2. Quan (化权) - Power

Nature: Authority, power, competition, control

  • Represents authority, status, reputation
  • Represents competition, rivalry, disputes
  • Represents leadership abilities and desire for control
  • Represents career advancement and power struggles

3. Ke (化科) - Noble

Nature: Fame, education, mentors, noble help

  • Represents fame, academic achievements
  • Represents mentors and helpful people
  • Represents exams, diplomas, certifications
  • Represents public recognition and reputation

4. Ji (化忌) - Obstacle

Nature: Obstacles, losses, attachment, difficulties

  • Represents obstacles, setbacks, challenges
  • Represents losses, financial problems
  • Represents obsession, stubbornness, attachment
  • Represents restriction, limitations

How Si Hua Affects the Chart

When a star transforms into one of the Four Transformations, it changes the energy of that palace. The location of Si Hua in your chart determines which areas of life are affected:

  • Lu in Career Palace: Career success, financial gains
  • Quan in Wealth Palace: Financial power, control of resources
  • Ke in Knowledge Palace: Academic achievements, mentor luck
  • Ji in Relationship Palace: Relationship challenges
