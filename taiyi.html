Tai Yi Shen Shu (太乙神数)
The Great Ultimate Numbers — Cosmic Cycle Divination

Where Da Liu Ren reads the day and Xuan Kong reads the space, Tai Yi reads the great sweeping cycles of cosmic time — and their impact on human destiny.

Of the three systems known collectively as The Three Greats (三式), Tai Yi Shen Shu (太乙神数 — "The Great Ultimate Numbers") is the most cosmic, the most abstract, and — for those with the patience to master it — the most powerfully predictive. Where Da Liu Ren begins its analysis from the day of the reading and Xuan Kong maps the temporal movement of energy through space, Tai Yi begins from the position of specific celestial bodies on a grand cyclical clock that stretches across centuries. It is a system designed to read the movement of eras — the coming and going of cycles of fortune and crisis that affect not individuals but nations, institutions, and the broader sweep of human events.

What Is Tai Yi?

Tai Yi is built on the premise that the movement of the cosmos operates in vast, predictable cycles — cycles that shape the fortune of dynasties, the rise and fall of institutions, and the broader environmental conditions within which individual lives unfold. It draws its data from the cyclical positions of the sun, moon, and specific planets — particularly Jupiter (the Year Star, 岁星) and Saturn (the Binding Star, 镇星) — and maps these positions onto a nine-palace framework similar to but distinct from Xuan Kong's.

The word Tai Yi (太乙) means "Great Ultimate" — the primordial unity from which the two polar energies of yin and yang emerge. This naming reflects the system's fundamental orientation: it seeks to understand the underlying unified pattern behind apparently random events, to see the coherent shape of history as it unfolds. Practitioners of Tai Yi learn to read not just whether a period will be favorable or challenging, but what type of energy characterizes it — whether it is a period suited to military action or to cultivation, to expansion or to consolidation.

The Nine Palaces and Tai Yi

Tai Yi's nine-palace structure (太乙九宫) shares the same Luo Shu arrangement as Xuan Kong but is read differently. In Tai Yi, the nine palaces represent nine distinct life domains or governance categories, and the movement of the cosmic cycle through these palaces determines the relative favorability of each domain in any given time period.

The movement of the Tai Yi cycle through these nine palaces follows a 360-year grand cycle divided into smaller sub-cycles. Within each sub-cycle, the cosmic energy highlights different palaces in sequence, creating periods of relative activation and dormancy. Understanding which palace is currently in command (得令) tells you the primary energetic character of the present era.

Tracking the Cosmic Cycle

Tai Yi's predictive power rests on its ability to track multiple simultaneous cycles and determine their confluence. The system monitors:

The Tai Yi cycle (太乙循环) — the primary 360-year cycle divided into 72 smaller five-year segments, each governed by a different configuration of palace energies. The Heavenly Stems cycle (天干循环) — the ten-day cycle that modifies the elemental quality of the current period. The Earthly Branches cycle (地支循环) — the twelve-year Jupiter cycle that identifies the year's general energetic character. And the Moon cycle (月令) — the twelve-month cycle that modulates the overall energy month by month.

When two or more cycles converge on the same palace or the same elemental quality simultaneously, the resulting energy is intensified. A year in which both the Jupiter cycle and the Tai Yi cycle highlight the same life domain will be dramatically more impactful for that domain than one in which only one cycle is active. This confluence analysis is one of Tai Yi's most distinctive and powerful features.

"Tai Yi reads the heartbeat of the cosmos. Where an individual's birth chart tells you who they are, the Tai Yi cycle tells you what kind of world they were born into — and whether the energy of their time runs with them or against them."

64 Configurations and Their Meanings

When the multiple cycles of Tai Yi are mapped onto the nine-palace structure, they generate what practitioners call the 64 configurations (64局). Each configuration represents a specific alignment of cosmic energies and carries a documented set of typical outcomes — what kinds of events are likely to occur, which domains are favored, and which are at risk during that configuration's active period.

These 64 configurations are the closest analog in Tai Yi to the hexagrams of the Yi Jing or the house configurations of Xuan Kong. They provide the foundational interpretive framework from which the practitioner reads the specific question. Unlike a hexagram, which answers a specific question, a configuration describes the environment within which events are occurring — the larger story of which the specific question is a chapter.

Military and Strategic Applications

Historically, Tai Yi was among the most closely guarded of imperial divination systems precisely because of its application to military planning. Generals and emperors used it to determine not only whether to go to war, but when to move, which directions to approach from, and what kind of opposition they would encounter. The system's ability to identify periods of cosmic advantage made it an invaluable strategic tool.

The war configuration (战局) analysis of Tai Yi was used to assess the relative strength of competing forces by mapping the cosmic cycle position of each side. The side whose position in the current cycle is more activated — whose palace domain is currently in command — carries the advantage of the era's energy. This does not guarantee victory (individual human factors remain decisive), but it means that the environment is more receptive to that side's actions during that period.

Today, this strategic dimension translates to applications in business competition, institutional planning, and political forecasting. Understanding which cycles favor your position and which favor your competitor's allows for better timing of major initiatives, negotiations, and strategic pivots.

Personal Destiny and Tai Yi

While Tai Yi is most powerful at the level of era and institution, it is not irrelevant to individual destiny. The cosmic cycles it tracks create the environmental context within which individual Ba Zi charts operate. A person born during an especially favorable Tai Yi configuration for their specific life domain enters the world at a moment when the broader cosmic energies are supportive of their particular type of purpose.

This integration between Tai Yi's macro-analysis and Ba Zi's micro-analysis is one of the most sophisticated applications of Chinese metaphysics: using Tai Yi to understand the broader era, Da Liu Ren to understand the day's energy, and Ba Zi to understand the individual's natal chart — read together, these systems provide a multi-layered picture of how the individual moves through time.

Getting Started with Tai Yi

Begin Your Journey into Cosmic Cycle Analysis

  1. Understand the 360-year grand cycle — Tai Yi's fundamental cycle spans 360 years, divided into 72 five-year segments. Within each segment, a different palace is in command. Identify which segment of the current cycle we are in.
  2. Track the Jupiter cycle — Jupiter (岁星) completes one orbit of the zodiac in approximately 12 years, moving through one Earthly Branch per year. This is the foundation of the annual forecast in Tai Yi. Know where Jupiter is now.
  3. Learn the nine palace domains — Each palace governs a specific life or governance domain. When the active cycle enters a specific palace, that domain is highlighted — favorable for that domain's activities, challenging for opposing domains.
  4. Study the 64 configurations — Each configuration is a specific alignment of the multiple cycles. Learn to identify the current configuration and read its documented implications for the present period.
  5. Apply confluence analysis — When two or more cycles point to the same palace or elemental quality simultaneously, the energy is amplified. Watch for these convergences as the highest-signal periods in the cycle.
  6. Integrate with Ba Zi and Da Liu Ren — Tai Yi provides the macro picture; Ba Zi the personal picture; Da Liu Ren the daily picture. Together they offer a complete multi-scale analysis of any situation.
