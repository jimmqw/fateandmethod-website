Why It Was Kept Secret

Tai Yi was considered too powerful ‚Ä?and too destabilizing ‚Ä?for general knowledge. A system that could predict the fall of a dynasty was a system that could be used to plan that fall. Consequently, Tai Yi knowledge was restricted to a small circle of imperial advisors and court historians.

This secrecy created a paradox: because it was rarely taught, it was rarely perfected. While Zi Wei and I Ching developed rich interpretive traditions through centuries of broad practice, Tai Yi remained narrow ‚Ä?its calculations precise but its interpretive frameworks underdeveloped compared to the other systems.

Today, this rarity is compounded for English speakers. Virtually no comprehensive Tai Yi resources exist in Western languages. The system remains in the hands of a handful of Chinese-language masters, most of whom are in their 70s and 80s and have not yet transmitted their knowledge widely.
