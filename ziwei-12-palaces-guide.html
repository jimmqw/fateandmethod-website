Ziwei 12 Palaces

The complete map of Ziwei Dou Shou's twelve life palaces — each a domain of your destiny, together forming the full portrait of your life path.

The 12 palaces (十二宫, Shi Er Gong) form the structural framework of every Ziwei chart. Think of them as twelve rooms in a house — each one represents a distinct life domain, and the stars that inhabit each room determine how that area of your life unfolds. Understanding the palaces is the key to reading a Ziwei chart with precision.

Unlike Western astrology where planets simply sign-hop, Ziwei places stars into palaces based on your exact birth time, creating a unique spatial map. Where a star falls — and which stars surround it — changes everything.

How to Use This Guide

Each palace below covers its core domain, which stars most commonly appear there, and what an empty palace (no major stars) means in practice. The palace's condition is read in combination with its ruling stars, your Day Master's strength, and the current Da Xian (10-year luck cycle) period. No single factor tells the whole story — Ziwei is a system of layered interpretation.

Life Palace

命宫 — Ming Gong

The core of your identity — your personality, willpower, and how the world perceives you.

The Life Palace is the most important palace in the Ziwei chart. It describes your core self: your personality traits, willpower, resilience, and the quality of your physical life. The stars that occupy your Life Palace define your fundamental character — whether you are naturally bold or cautious, ambitious or laid-back, emotionally expressive or reserved.

When Ziwei itself sits in the Life Palace, it indicates exceptional leadership potential and strong personal authority. When the Life Palace is empty of major stars, it suggests a more adaptable, less fixed personality — someone who can reinvent themselves across different life phases.

  • Strong Life Palace: Clear self-identity, strong willpower, consistent life direction
  • Challenged Life Palace: Identity struggles, periods of confusion, fluctuating self-image
  • Key stars here: Ziwei, Tianji, Tianxiang — each gives a distinctly different core character

Siblings Palace

兄弟宫 — Xiong Di Gong

Your relationships with siblings, peers, and the role of cooperation in your life.

The Siblings Palace describes your relationship with brothers and sisters (or siblings in a broader sense — close peers and equals). Beyond literal sibling dynamics, this palace also reflects your ability to cooperate, share resources, and function as part of a team.

In modern interpretation, the Siblings Palace also speaks to your peer network — close friends who function as quasi-siblings, colleagues who become trusted allies, and the quality of collaborative relationships in your life.

  • Strong Siblings Palace: Supportive sibling relationships, strong peer network, good teamwork ability
  • Empty or challenged: Sibling estrangement, competitive rather than cooperative peer relationships, or a largely solitary life
  • Note: In charts without siblings, this palace still reflects peer relationships and cooperative dynamics
3

Property Palace

田宅宫 — Tian Zhai Gong

Your connection to property, home, family roots, and inherited environment.

The Property Palace governs your relationship with real estate, land, the physical home environment, and your family lineage. It describes not just whether you will own property, but the quality and nature of your domestic situation — the stability of your home life, your relationship with your parents and ancestors, and the environment you create for yourself and your family.

This palace also reflects the "roots" dimension of your life — the cultural, familial, and environmental heritage you carry. In modern terms, it speaks to your sense of belonging and the foundation from which you operate in the world.

  • Strong Property Palace: Early home ownership, stable family environment, harmonious household
  • Challenged: Frequent moving, unstable home life, property disputes, or estrangement from family roots
  • Related reading: Pair with the Parents Palace (10) for a complete picture of family inheritance and lineage
4

Career Palace

官禄宫 — Guan Lu Gong

Your career direction, public status, academic achievement, and professional reputation.

The Career Palace (官禄宫) describes your professional path — the type of work you are naturally drawn to, how you build your reputation, and the kind of status you achieve in the world. This is one of the most practically useful palaces for life planning: understanding your Career Palace helps you choose industries, roles, and career strategies aligned with your chart's natural bent.

In some interpretations, the Career Palace and Business Palace (9) work as a pair — Career governs employment and formal career tracks, while Business governs entrepreneurship and wealth-building through commerce. Together they map your entire economic life.

  • Strong Career Palace: Clear career direction, recognition, steady advancement, strong professional reputation
  • Challenged: Career confusion, frequent job changes, difficulty gaining recognition, or underemployment relative to abilities
  • Key question: Which stars sit here, and are they in their "home" or "exalted" positions? This determines ease or struggle in career matters
5

Friends Palace

交友宫 — Jiao You Gong

Your broader social network, casual friends, and the quality of your social luck.

The Friends Palace describes the wider circle of people in your life — not close intimates (those belong to the Spouse Palace or Siblings Palace) but the network of acquaintances, friends, colleagues, and contacts that form your social environment. This palace reflects the "social luck" dimension of your chart: the quality of people who come into your life, how useful your network is, and how you are treated by the broader world.

A strong Friends Palace suggests a wide, helpful network — people who open doors, provide opportunities, and support your goals. A challenged Friends Palace may indicate network that is superficial, unreliable, or that costs more than it gives.

  • Strong: Wide helpful network, doors open through connections, positive social luck
  • Empty: More independent path, limited network benefit, or social isolation in certain cycles
  • Note: This is different from the Servants Palace (8), which governs subordinates and hired help
6

Health Palace

疾厄宫 — Ji Yi Gong

Your health tendencies, vulnerability patterns, and the body's natural weak points.

The Health Palace identifies the body's natural vulnerabilities — which organ systems and life areas are most at risk under stress. This is one of the most practically valuable palaces in Ziwei: understanding your Health Palace allows you to take preventive measures and know which periods of your life require extra health vigilance.

Ziwei practitioners read the Health Palace in combination with the Da Xian cycles: when a challenged period overlaps with a vulnerable Health Palace, it signals a time when extra self-care is essential. This palace also reflects your body's constitution — whether you are naturally robust or delicate.

  • Strong Health Palace: Generally robust constitution, fewer health issues across the lifespan
  • Challenged: Specific vulnerabilities (e.g., digestive, respiratory, nervous system) that require attention in certain cycles
  • Important: Always read the Health Palace alongside the current Da Xian — a challenged Health Palace does not guarantee illness, only vulnerability during difficult cycles
7

Migration Palace

迁移宮 — Qian Yi Gong

Travel, migration, relocation, and how you are perceived by the outside world.

The Migration Palace governs travel (both short trips and long-distance migration), how you are received in external environments, and the impression you make on strangers. This is one of the more externally focused palaces: it describes your "packaging" — how you present yourself to the world when you are outside your home territory.

For those considering emigration or frequent travel for work, the Migration Palace provides essential guidance. A strong Migration Palace often indicates ease of movement, positive reception in foreign environments, and the ability to adapt to new cultures. A challenged Migration Palace may suggest difficulties with travel, homesickness, or being perceived unfavorably in external settings.

  • Strong: Favorable travel luck, positive reception abroad, ease of relocation
  • Challenged: Travel obstacles, cultural friction abroad, or feeling like an outsider
  • Modern reading: Also governs your public image and how you are perceived in social settings outside your close circle
8

Servants Palace

奴僕宮 — Nu Pu Gong

Subordinates, employees, hired help, and the management of those in lesser positions.

The Servants Palace governs your relationship with subordinates — employees, assistants, contractors, and anyone who works under your direction. In modern professional terms, this palace describes your management style, how well you are served by those you employ, and the quality of support you receive from those in positions below yours.

A strong Servants Palace suggests loyalty from subordinates, effective delegation, and a healthy management relationship. A challenged Servants Palace may indicate high turnover, difficulty retaining good staff, or being exploited by those in service roles.

  • Strong: Loyal, capable subordinates; smooth management relationships; good delegation ability
  • Challenged: Staff conflicts, high turnover, exploitation by business partners or hired help
  • Note: This is distinct from the Friends Palace (5), which governs peers and equals
9

Business Palace

官禄宫 — Guan Lu Gong (Financial)

Financial career, business ventures, investment capacity, and wealth accumulation.

The Business Palace works in tandem with the Career Palace (4) to map your entire economic life. While Career governs formal employment and professional status, the Business Palace governs entrepreneurship, investment, and the ability to generate wealth through business activities. If you have a strong Business Palace, you are likely suited for business ownership, investment, or financial speculation. If it is challenged, a stable employment career may be more suitable than risky ventures.

Reading the Business Palace alongside the Wealth star placement and your elemental chart balance gives a complete picture of your financial temperament — whether you build wealth through steady accumulation or bold risk, through your own business or through financial instruments.

  • Strong: Good business instincts, investment luck, ability to build wealth through commerce
  • Challenged: Business setbacks, poor investment timing, or更适合 employment rather than entrepreneurship
  • Related: Pair with Career Palace (4) and the Flying Star chart's wealth sectors for full financial picture

Parents Palace

父母宮 — Fu Mu Gong

Your relationship with parents, authority figures, and the sources of your worldly protection.

The Parents Palace describes your relationship with your parents and parental figures — the people who provided for you, protected you, and shaped your early environment. In a broader sense, it also governs your relationship with authority: bosses, mentors, institutions, and the formal structures of the world that either support or challenge you.

This palace also describes what you "inherit" from your parents — not just genetically, but in terms of environment, education, social position, and the psychological patterns passed down through family systems. A strong Parents Palace suggests supportive parents and easy access to institutional favor; a challenged one may indicate parent estrangement, early loss, or conflict with authority throughout life.

  • Strong: Supportive parental relationships, easy access to institutional support, positive relationship with authority
  • Challenged: Parent estrangement or loss, conflict with authority, or having to build without family support
  • Important: Also governs your relationship with your own role as an authority figure — parents, bosses, or institutional leaders
11

Virtue Palace

福德宫 — Fu De Gong

Your happiness, lifestyle quality, spiritual practice, and the merit you accumulate.

The Virtue Palace (福德宫) describes your quality of life — not just material comfort, but your inner experience of happiness, contentment, and wellbeing. It governs your lifestyle, your spiritual or religious inclinations, and the "merit" (福, fu) that accumulates through virtuous living. This is one of the more introspective palaces: it speaks to what actually satisfies you at a deep level, beyond external achievements.

A strong Virtue Palace suggests a life with genuine enjoyment, the ability to appreciate what one has, and a certain spiritual ease. A challenged Virtue Palace may indicate chronic dissatisfaction, difficulty experiencing contentment, or a life that looks successful from outside but feels empty within. This palace is particularly important in later life Da Xian periods, when it increasingly governs overall life satisfaction.

  • Strong: Genuine happiness, contentment, appreciation of life's pleasures, spiritual ease
  • Challenged: Chronic dissatisfaction, hedonic adaptation (never satisfied), or spiritual restlessness
  • Modern reading: Also governs your lifestyle aesthetic, recreation habits, and the quality of daily life
12

Children Palace

子息宮 — Zi Xi Gong

Children, creative output, artistic expression, and the legacy you leave behind.

The Children Palace governs your relationship with children — whether you have them, how you relate to them, and the nature of that bond. But this palace has a second, equally important meaning: it governs creative expression and the legacy dimension of life. In modern terms, the Children Palace describes not just parenthood, but anything you create that outlives you — art, writing, businesses you build, ideas you leave behind.

This dual meaning makes the Children Palace one of the most personally meaningful palaces in the chart. A strong Children Palace suggests creative vitality and the potential for meaningful legacy. A challenged one may indicate difficulty with children, or creative blocks that prevent self-expression — but "children" here is always read in context with the rest of the chart and the individual's actual life choices.

  • Strong: Strong creative output, good relationship with children, lasting legacy potential
  • Challenged: Creative blocks, difficulty with children, or weak legacy motivation
  • Key insight: Even childless individuals have a Children Palace — it describes creative drive and the desire to make something that lasts

How Palaces Interact: Reading the Chart as a Whole

The real power of Ziwei comes from reading palaces in relation to each other. A star in the Career Palace that also "looks at" (三方四正 interaction) the Wealth Palace creates a different picture than the same star sitting in isolation. The Life Palace and Career Palace relationship tells you whether your work aligns with your identity. The Parents Palace and Property Palace together map your family inheritance — both material and psychological.

The Empty Palace Signal

An empty palace — one with no major stars — is not a neutral finding. In Ziwei, empty palaces often indicate that the corresponding life area is less fated or more subject to free will. You have more choice in that domain, but also less automatic support. Empty palaces can be powerful or challenging depending on which luck cycle you are in. During a Da Xian that activates an empty palace, that life area typically undergoes significant change — for better or worse depending on the overall chart strength.

The 12 palaces together create a complete map of your life's territories. No single palace tells your full story — the Ziwei chart reveals itself through the relationships between palaces, the stars within them, and how they activate across your 10-year luck cycles. Use this guide as a foundation, then explore how your specific stars interact with each palace in your own chart.
