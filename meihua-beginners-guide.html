Plum Blossom Divination Without Coins — A Mei Hua Yi Shu Beginner's Guide

How to generate I Ching hexagrams spontaneously from numbers in your environment — dates, sounds, directions, and words — and interpret them through the Eight Trigrams, no tools required.

Mei Hua Yi Shu (梅花易数) — "Plum Blossom Numbers" — is the most elegant and spontaneous form of I Ching divination. Unlike Liu Yao's coin tossing or the classical text's book consultation, Mei Hua derives hexagrams from the numbers already present around you: the current date and time, the direction a bird flies, the number of words someone speaks, the knock at a door. This guide teaches you to generate and interpret hexagrams using nothing but observation and simple arithmetic — no coins, no yarrow stalks, no books.

01 · What Is Mei Hua Yi Shu?

Mei Hua Yi Shu was systematized by the Northern Song Dynasty scholar Shao Yong (邵雍, 1011–1077), one of the greatest minds in Chinese intellectual history. Shao Yong was fascinated by the mathematical structure underlying the I Ching, and he recognized that the 64 hexagrams were not just divination symbols — they were a complete map of transformation, capable of describing any situation in terms of the interaction between two three-line trigrams and their changing lines.

The name "Plum Blossom" comes from a famous legend: Shao Yong was observing plum blossoms in his garden when two sparrows fought on a branch and fell to the ground. He calculated the date and time of the event, derived a hexagram, and correctly predicted that a neighbor girl would come to pick blossoms and be injured — but not seriously. The prediction proved true the following evening, and the method was named after the plum tree that occasioned its first verified use.

What makes Mei Hua distinct among I Ching methods is its speed. While Liu Yao requires six coin tosses and reference to a sexagenary calendar, Mei Hua can generate a hexagram in seconds from ambient numbers. A practitioner walking down a street can derive a reading from the address number of a building they pass, the number of cars waiting at a light, or the digits of a clock.

02 · The Eight Trigrams — Your Building Blocks

Before generating hexagrams, you must know the Eight Trigrams (八卦, Ba Gua) and their numerical assignments. Every hexagram is composed of two trigrams — an upper (outer) trigram and a lower (inner) trigram. In Mei Hua, numbers 1 through 8 correspond to specific trigrams.

Each trigram carries rich associations — its natural phenomenon, element, family role, direction, season, body part, and animal. When you derive a hexagram in Mei Hua, you're not just looking at lines — you're looking at two natural forces interacting.

03 · Three Methods to Generate a Hexagram

The core of Mei Hua is simple: you obtain three numbers, then apply simple arithmetic to derive the upper trigram, lower trigram, and changing line. Here are the three most reliable methods:

Method 1: Date and Time (The Most Reliable)

Date-Based Hexagram Generation

Convert the current date and time to Chinese lunar calendar numbers:

  • Year number: The last two digits of the lunar year or the Chinese sexagenary cycle year number (2026 = year 43 in the cycle)
  • Month number: The lunar month (1–12)
  • Day number: The lunar day (1–30)
  • Hour number: The Chinese double-hour (子 23-1 = 1, 丑 1-3 = 2, … 亥 21-23 = 12)

Upper trigram = (Year + Month + Day) ÷ 8, take the remainder (0 becomes 8)
Lower trigram = (Year + Month + Day + Hour) ÷ 8, take the remainder (0 becomes 8)
Changing line = (Year + Month + Day + Hour) ÷ 6, take the remainder (0 becomes 6)

Method 2: Environmental Numbers

Observe your environment for numbers. Count the people in a room. Note a license plate. Use an address number. The three numbers can be any sequence you notice — three digits from a clock (e.g., 14:27:09 → 14, 27, 9), three objects you see (2 birds, 5 trees, 1 bench), or three sounds you hear (3 knocks, 1 car horn, 2 footsteps).

Upper trigram = first number ÷ 8, remainder → trigram
Lower trigram = second number ÷ 8, remainder → trigram
Changing line = third number ÷ 6, remainder → line position

Method 3: Direction + Time (For Mobile Reading)

When you're outdoors, use the direction you're facing or walking toward as the upper trigram, and the direction something approaches from as the lower trigram. Match the direction to the trigram table above. Use the current hour number for the changing line.

Important: Regardless of method, always divide by 8 for trigrams and by 6 for the changing line. The remainder determines the result. If the remainder is 0, use 8 (for trigrams) or 6 (for changing line).

04 · A Complete Worked Example

Example: April 27, 2026, at 14:00 (2 PM)

Step 1 — Convert to numbers:

  • Year: 2026 → use 26 (last two digits)
  • Month: April → 4
  • Day: 27
  • Hour: 14:00 → Chinese double-hour 未 (Wei) → 8

Step 2 — Calculate upper trigram:

(26 + 4 + 27) = 57. 57 ÷ 8 = 7 remainder 1 → Trigram 1 = ☰ Qian (Heaven)

Step 3 — Calculate lower trigram:

(57 + 8) = 65. 65 ÷ 8 = 8 remainder 1 → Trigram 1 = ☰ Qian (Heaven)

Step 4 — Calculate changing line:

65 ÷ 6 = 10 remainder 5 → Line 5 is changing

Result: Hexagram 1 — ䷀ Qian / The Creative, with Line 5 changing → ䷍ Da You / Great Possessing

05 · How to Interpret the Reading

Mei Hua interpretation follows a layered approach, moving from the largest structure to the finest detail:

The Five-Layer Interpretation Method

  1. Upper and Lower Trigram Relationship. What two natural forces are interacting? Heaven over Fire means something very different from Mountain over Water. Read the trigrams as a situation, not just symbols.
  2. The Hexagram's Theme. What is the name and traditional meaning of the hexagram? Even a one-sentence summary ("Approach," "Obstruction," "Waiting") provides the overall tone.
  3. The Changing Line's Message. Read the specific text for the changing line. This is the precise advice of the reading.
  4. The Transformed Hexagram. What hexagram does the original become after the line changes? This shows the outcome or future direction.
  5. Mutual (Interlocking) Trigrams. Take the original hexagram's lines 2-3-4 as the lower trigram and lines 3-4-5 as the upper trigram. This inner hexagram reveals the hidden dynamics at play.

Let's walk through Layer 1 with our example. Qian (Heaven) over Qian (Heaven) → pure creative energy, initiative, the beginning of a cycle. Line 5 changing from Yang to Yin transforms the upper trigram from Qian to Li (Fire), producing Hexagram 14, Da You (Great Possessing) — Fire over Heaven, abundance after effort, the harvest. The reading says: you are in a period of pure initiative, and at the leadership level (Line 5 is the ruler's position), a shift is occurring from active leadership to illuminating others — from doing to sharing.

06 · Trigram Relationships — The Shortcut to Speed

Once you internalize the Eight Trigrams, you can produce a rough reading in seconds by checking only two things: the elemental relationship between the two trigrams and the changing line's position.

07 · When to Use Mei Hua vs. Other Methods

Mei Hua excels at immediate, situational questions — the kind that arise spontaneously during your day: "Should I take this meeting?" "Is this the right moment to make that call?" "What is the nature of the person I just met?" Because it requires no tools, it's the method of choice when you need insight now.

For questions involving longer timelines — career trajectory over two years, whether to relocate across the country, the outcome of a complex legal matter — Liu Yao or Zi Wei Dou Shu may provide richer detail. Mei Hua is at its best with clearly bounded, immediate situations where the environment itself offers the numbers for the consultation.

08 · Conclusion

Mei Hua Yi Shu is perhaps the most philosophically satisfying of the I Ching methods because it expresses the core Daoist insight: that the macrocosm and microcosm are one, that the patterns of the universe are present in every moment and every number. When you derive a hexagram from the date and time of a question, you are not "creating" a random symbol — you are recognizing the hexagram that was already latent in that moment.

Shao Yong himself wrote: "The mind of Heaven and Earth is revealed in numbers." What he meant was not that numbers are mystical, but that numbers structure reality, and the I Ching's genius was to map that structure into 64 symbols that any human mind can read.

Your practice: carry a small journal. For one week, generate one Mei Hua hexagram each morning from the date alone, and note the theme. At the end of each day, reflect on whether the theme resonated. After seven days, you'll have a personal relationship with the Eight Trigrams that no book could provide — and the Plum Blossom will have begun to bloom in your own understanding.
