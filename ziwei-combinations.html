Zi Wei Dou Shu  /  Star Combinations

How the 14 Stars Interact

The real art of Zi Wei Dou Shu: when two stars share a palace, their combination creates a meaning neither star has alone.

A single star, placed alone in a palace, tells you something. But in real Zi Wei charts, almost every palace contains multiple stars — and the way these stars interact is what creates the chart's depth and nuance. Understanding star combinations (星曜组合) is where the beginner's knowledge becomes the reader's art. This is the level at which Zi Wei Dou Shu stops being a list of traits and becomes a genuine personality map.

Why Combinations Matter More Than Single Stars

Think of it this way: a single star in a palace is like a single instrument playing a note. The combination of two or more stars in the same palace is like those instruments playing together — creating a chord whose character is different from any individual note. A 紫微 (Emperor Star) alone in your Life Palace suggests ambition and authority. Add 天相 (Prime Minister Star) to the same palace, and suddenly you have the Emperor with a capable minister: the combination means someone who can execute authority with diplomacy, not just wield it.

Similarly, 贪狼 (Greedy Wolf Star) alone suggests powerful desire and social ambition. Add 廉贞 (Pure Chastity Star) to the same palace, and the combination creates something more complex: desire tempered by deep feeling, ambition refined by emotional intelligence.

The Six Combination Principles

In classical Zi Wei practice, star combinations are governed by six principles:

1. Alone is best (单星独守): A star alone in a palace expresses its pure nature most clearly. The more stars in a palace, the more diluted — or transformed — each star's expression becomes.

2. Dominant-dominant (双星叠临): When two major stars occupy the same palace, one is always stronger. The dominant star sets the overall tone, while the secondary star modifies and nuances it.

3. Good-good combination (星系相生): When stars that support each other's nature share a palace, they amplify each other's positive qualities.

4. Good-bad combination (星系相克): When stars with conflicting natures share a palace, the tension can produce either destruction or extraordinary dynamism, depending on which star is dominant.

5. Empty palace (宫位为空): An empty palace — one with few or no major stars — does not mean the life domain is absent. It means the energy is dormant, available to be activated by favorable transits or deliberate cultivation.

6. Palace at its strongest (宫强则显): A palace containing multiple major stars becomes the dominant arena of life. If your Wealth Palace has five major stars, your financial life will be the central arena of your existence.

Five Key Combinations to Know

1. 紫微 + 天府 — The Emperor and the Treasurer

This is one of the most favorable combinations in the entire system. 紫微 brings ambition, leadership, and the desire to be exceptional. 天府 brings the ability to steward, accumulate, and protect what is built. Together: a person of genuine authority who accumulates wealth and status through principled means. This combination is often found in the charts of successful entrepreneurs, senior executives, and public figures who have built lasting legacies.

2. 紫微 + 天相 — The Emperor and the Prime Minister

Where 紫微 alone can become authoritarian, adding 天相 brings diplomatic skill and the ability to lead through consensus rather than command. This combination is classic for people in advisory roles, senior management, and anyone whose authority depends on the support of others rather than raw power.

3. 贪狼 + 廉贞 — Desire and Passion

The most romantically charged combination in Zi Wei. 贪狼 brings the Greedy Wolf's desire, social intelligence, and ambition. 廉贞 adds depth of feeling, intensity, and the capacity for profound emotional attachment. This combination creates extraordinary charisma and deep romantic capacity — but also the potential for obsessive relationships, jealousy, and emotional volatility. In the right configuration, this is the combination of the passionate artist or devoted lover. In the wrong configuration, it is the combination of the romantic disaster.

4. 太阳 + 太阴 — The Sun and the Moon

The yin-yang balance in action. 太阳 is outward, radiant, public-facing, generous. 太阴 is inward, intuitive, nurturing, private. When these two share a palace, you get someone who can shift between these modes as circumstances require — outwardly bright and publicly generous, inwardly deep and emotionally intelligent. This is one of the most balanced and harmonious combinations in the system.

5. 七杀 + 破军 — The Two Destroyers

The most extreme combination: two of the most powerful and dangerous stars in the system occupying the same palace. This combination is associated with military generals, revolutionary leaders, and people who change the world through radical destruction and rebuilding. In everyday life, it can manifest as a person who lives at extraordinary intensity — careers that burn brightly, relationships that transform completely, lives that rarely go at half speed. This combination requires exceptionally strong supporting stars in other palaces to balance it.

The Four Transformations: Adding Another Dimension

Star combinations become even more complex when you add the Four Transformations (四化):

Each star can be activated by one of four transformations — 禄 (abundance), 权 (power), 科 (knowledge), or 忌 (obstacle) — which fundamentally changes its expression in that palace. A 贪狼 without transformation is a strong desire. A 贪狼化禄 is desire that converts directly to financial or social abundance. A 贪狼化忌 is desire that creates its own obstacles — obsessive, potentially destructive attachment.

How to Practice Reading Combinations

When reading a Zi Wei chart, start with these questions for each palace:

1. How many major stars does this palace contain?
2. Which stars are present and what is their natural relationship?
3. Which star is dominant?
4. Are there Four Transformations active in this palace?
5. What life domain does this palace govern?

The answer to these five questions gives you a nuanced reading of that life domain that is far more accurate than single-star interpretation alone.

"A chart is not a collection of stars. It is a symphony. The reader who understands combinations hears the music — and the reader who only knows individual stars hears only noise."
