The Three Greats — Pinnacle of Chinese Metaphysics

Among the nine systems on this site, three stand apart in depth, complexity, and historical prestige. Known as The Three Greats (三式), these systems — Da Liu Ren, Xuan Kong, and Tai Yi — were considered so powerful and so profound that for centuries they were restricted to imperial use alone.

Together they form a complete metaphysical triad: Da Liu Ren addresses heavenly timing (天时) — the cosmic currents that shape destiny; Xuan Kong addresses spatial timing (空间) — how the energy of place combines with time; and Tai Yi addresses human affairs (人事) — the unfolding of relationships and events.

Astrology & Birth Destiny

The Chinese astrological tradition spans systems that read the sky at birth (Zi Wei Dou Shu) and systems that read the birth moment's energetic signature (Ba Zi). Both are built on the same foundational vocabulary — the Ten Heavenly Stems and Twelve Earthly Branches — but they apply it differently.

Zi Wei Dou Shu maps your entire life through a natal chart organized around twelve palaces, reading the interactions between stars rather than planetary positions. Ba Zi distills your birth moment into four pillars and reads the flow of elemental energy through your life decade by decade.

Divination — Direct Answers from the Universe

The divination systems on this site offer direct answers to specific questions. Liu Yao uses three coins thrown six times to generate a hexagram — precise, structured, and centuries-validated. Mei Hua Yi Shu is more spontaneous, drawing meaning from numbers, characters, timing, or even the texture of a conversation. Xiao Liu Ren offers rapid three-number answers when a full reading is impractical.

Space & Time — Xuan Kong

Feng shui is widely known in the West, but Xuan Kong (玄空风水) represents its most sophisticated and analytical dimension. Rather than static compass directions, Xuan Kong tracks how the energy of a space changes over time — which areas of a building are activated or challenged in any given year, month, or day. It is feng shui as a living, temporal system rather than a static one.
